<ul>
<li><strong>Extra heart sound</strong>: This means that there is an additional sound in the heart that is not part of the normal cardiac cycle, which may be due to various conditions such as pericarditis, pleural effusion, pneumothorax, or cardiac tamponade. The medical advice for this category depends on the cause and severity of the extra sound.&nbsp;Some possible treatments are antibiotics for infections, drainage for fluid accumulation, surgery for air leakage or pressure buildup.</li>
</ul>
<p>Please consult a doctor for accurate evaluation and diagnosis. Tests used to identify additional heart sounds include electrocardiogram (ECG), echocardiogram, and various other tests depending on the case.</p>
